Jim Campbell

Jim Campbell has over three decades of communication experience in both non-commercial broadcasting and corporate communication. One of his special areas of interest and expertise is bringing an understanding of science issues and concerns to a wider public so that citizens can understand more fully the implications of public policy involving science and technology on everyday life, and can therefore make more informed decisions as they participate in the policy making process. In addition to serving as General Manager of non-commercial radio stations in markets such as Philadelphia and Buffalo, he has also written and taught at the college level, as well as produced over three thousand hours of radio and video programming, working with and interviewing scientists, musicians, writers, and others. His special areas of expertise are science, health care, and high technology topics. His print articles have appeared in general circulation periodicals, academic journals, and in specialized national trade publications. He has interviewed several Nobel Laureates in science, and his science interviews and other pieces have been broadcast on many local non-commercial radio services as well as on National Public Radio. Video pieces have appeared on PBS stations as well as major cable networks. His writing, producing, and editing has won awards for topics ranging from teenage smoking prevention to fiber optic technology to the video version of "The Old Farmer's Almanac." He holds degrees in History and American Civilization and, as a "maturity onset" graduate student, is currently working on a dissertation for his Ph.D. in Spatial information Science and Engineering.
